# Presentation Design Skill

## When to Use

When designing presentation structure, slide content, or speaking notes.

## Story Arc Templates

### Problem → Solution → Impact (most common)
1. **Hook**: Startling fact or question
2. **Problem**: Pain the audience feels
3. **Solution**: Your proposal
4. **Evidence**: Data/case studies
5. **Impact**: What changes if we act
6. **Ask**: What you need from them

### Situation → Complication → Resolution (McKinsey style)
1. **Situation**: What everyone agrees on
2. **Complication**: What changed or went wrong
3. **Resolution**: What to do about it

### What → So What → Now What (for data presentations)
1. **What**: The data/findings
2. **So What**: Why it matters
3. **Now What**: Recommended actions

## Slide Design Rules

- **One idea per slide**: If you need "and" in the title, split it
- **Action title**: Title = conclusion, not topic ("Revenue grew 15%" not "Revenue Overview")
- **6-word title rule**: Titles should be scannable (aim for 6-8 words max)
- **Max 3 bullets**: More than 3 points → split into multiple slides
- **Data ≠ decoration**: Every chart must have a clear takeaway

## Anti-Patterns

- **Reading slides aloud**: Slides are visual aids, not scripts
- **Data dump**: Showing every number instead of the key insight
- **Clipart/stock photos**: Use sparingly and purposefully
- **Animation overuse**: Transitions distract from content
