# Competitive Analysis Skill

## When to Use

When evaluating competitive positioning, comparing offerings, or identifying market opportunities and threats.

## Frameworks

### SWOT Analysis
| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

Key: Connect insights → "How can our Strengths exploit Opportunities?" / "How do our Weaknesses expose us to Threats?"

### Feature Comparison Matrix
| Feature | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---------|----|-----------:|-----------:|
| [Feature] | ✅ Full | ✅ Full | ⚠️ Partial |
| [Feature] | ❌ None | ✅ Full | ✅ Full |

### Differentiation Map
Plot competitors on two dimensions most important to customers (e.g., price vs. features, speed vs. accuracy).

## Data Sources
- Product websites, pricing pages, changelogs
- Review sites (G2, Capterra, ProductHunt)
- Job postings (reveal strategy through hiring patterns)
- Press releases, blog posts, social media
- SEC filings / funding announcements

## Anti-Patterns
- **Feature envy**: Don't assume competitor features are better — context matters
- **Outdated intel**: Competitive landscape changes fast — date all information
- **Confirmation bias**: Look for evidence that challenges your assumptions
