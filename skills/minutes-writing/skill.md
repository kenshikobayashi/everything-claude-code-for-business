# Minutes Writing Skill

## When to Use

When transforming raw meeting notes into structured, actionable meeting minutes.

## Core Principles

1. **Decisions are sacred**: Every decision must be captured with rationale
2. **Action items are non-negotiable**: Every action needs who, what, and by when
3. **Signal over noise**: Capture what matters, not everything that was said
4. **Written for absent people**: Someone who wasn't there should understand what happened

## Structure

```markdown
## Meeting Minutes: [Title]

**Date**: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM–HH:MM | **Location**: [Room/Link]
**Attendees**: [Present] | **Absent**: [Expected but absent]

### Decisions
1. **[Decision statement]** — Rationale: [why]

### Action Items
| # | Action | Owner | Due | Status |
|---|--------|-------|-----|--------|

### Discussion Summary
#### [Topic]
- Key points
- Perspectives raised
- How consensus was reached

### Parking Lot
- [Deferred items]

### Next Meeting
- Date: | Proposed agenda:
```

## Action Item Rules

Every action item MUST have:
- **What**: Specific, verifiable deliverable (not "look into X" but "analyze X and share findings")
- **Who**: Single owner (not "the team")
- **When**: Specific date (not "soon" or "next week")

## Checklist

- [ ] All decisions captured with rationale
- [ ] All action items have owner + due date
- [ ] Absent members identified (they need to be informed)
- [ ] Parking lot items recorded (nothing lost)
- [ ] Written in a way that someone absent can understand
