# Negotiation Preparation Skill

## When to Use

When preparing for any negotiation — vendor contracts, salary discussions, partnership deals, or internal resource allocation.

## Framework: BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)

1. **Our BATNA**: What do we do if this negotiation fails? (The stronger your BATNA, the stronger your position)
2. **Their BATNA**: What do they do if this fails? (Understanding their alternatives reveals their flexibility)
3. **ZOPA** (Zone of Possible Agreement): The overlap between our minimum and their maximum

## Preparation Checklist

```markdown
## Negotiation Prep: [Topic]

### Objectives
- **Ideal outcome**: [Best case]
- **Acceptable outcome**: [Minimum we'd accept]
- **Walk-away point**: [Below this, we leave]

### Our Position
- **BATNA**: [Our alternative if no deal]
- **Strengths**: [Leverage we have]
- **Weaknesses**: [Where we're vulnerable]

### Their Position (estimated)
- **BATNA**: [Their likely alternative]
- **Interests**: [What they really want — beyond stated positions]
- **Pressure points**: [Deadlines, budget cycles, competitive pressure]

### Issue Map
| Issue | Our Priority | Their Priority | Trade Potential |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Price | High | High | Link to volume/term |
| Timeline | Medium | High | Concede for price |
| Scope | High | Medium | Define clearly |

### Concession Strategy
- Give: [Things we can offer that cost us little]
- Get: [Things we need that may cost them little]
- Sequence: [What to offer first vs. hold back]

### Key Phrases
- Opening: "[Frame the discussion collaboratively]"
- If pushed: "[Redirect to interests, not positions]"
- Close: "[Summarize agreements, confirm next steps]"
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Revealing your BATNA if it's weak**: Only share if it strengthens your position
- **Positional bargaining**: Focus on interests, not positions ("I need X" → "I need to achieve Y")
- **Splitting the difference too early**: Leaves value on the table
