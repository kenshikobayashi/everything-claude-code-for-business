# 1-on-1 Meeting Skill

## When to Use

When preparing for, conducting, or following up on 1-on-1 meetings.

## Agenda Framework

```
1. Check-in (5min) — "How are you doing?"
2. Their topics (10min) — Their agenda first, always
3. Wins (5min) — Acknowledge recent accomplishments
4. Blockers (5min) — Problems they need help with
5. Growth (5min) — Career development, learning
6. Feedback (5min) — Both directions
7. Action items (2min) — Capture and confirm
```

## Question Bank

### Check-in
- "What's been on your mind this week?"
- "How are you feeling about your workload?"
- "Is there anything I can help unblock?"

### Growth
- "What skill do you want to develop next?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 12 months?"
- "What kind of projects energize you?"

### Feedback (asking for)
- "What's one thing I could do better as your manager?"
- "Is there anything about how our team works that frustrates you?"
- "Do you feel like you have everything you need to do your best work?"

## Feedback Framework: SBI

- **Situation**: Specific time and place
- **Behavior**: Observable action (not interpretation)
- **Impact**: Effect on you, the team, or the outcome

Example: "In yesterday's sprint review [S], when you presented the demo without testing it first [B], the bug we hit in front of stakeholders eroded their confidence in our timeline [I]."

## Anti-Patterns

- **Status update meeting**: 1-on-1s are for the person, not the project
- **Manager monologue**: The direct report should talk at least 50% of the time
- **Skipping regularly**: Canceling 1-on-1s signals they're not important
- **No notes**: Capture action items — follow through builds trust
