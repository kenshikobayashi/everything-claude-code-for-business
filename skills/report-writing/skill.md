# Report Writing Skill

## When to Use

When creating any recurring or one-off business report.

## Core Principles

1. **Outcomes over activities**: "Increased conversion by 15%" not "Worked on conversion optimization"
2. **Executive summary first**: The reader should get 80% of value from the first paragraph
3. **Data-driven**: Include metrics, comparisons, and trends
4. **Honest**: Don't hide bad news — surface blockers and risks early
5. **Actionable**: Every report should suggest what happens next

## Report Types

### Weekly Status

Focus: What happened, what's next, what's blocked.

```markdown
## Weekly Report: [Date Range]

### Highlights (top 3)
- [Outcome with metric]

### Completed | In Progress | Blocked
[Tables with status]

### Next Week
[Priorities]
```

### Monthly Business Report

Focus: Trends, goal progress, strategic adjustments.

```markdown
## Monthly Report: [Month]

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Target | Actual | MoM Change | Status |
[Data table]

### Goal Progress
[OKR/KPI updates]

### Highlights & Lowlights

### Strategic Adjustments
[What we're changing and why]
```

### Executive Summary

Focus: Business impact, decisions needed, risks.

```markdown
## Executive Summary: [Topic]

### Bottom Line
[1-2 sentences: the single most important thing]

### Key Numbers
[3-5 critical metrics]

### Decisions Needed
[What, by when, impact of delay]

### Risks
[Top 3 with mitigation status]
```

## Data Presentation Rules

- **Tables**: Use for structured comparisons (metrics, options)
- **Trends**: Always show period-over-period change (%, absolute, direction)
- **RAG status**: 🟢 Green (on track) / 🟡 Amber (at risk) / 🔴 Red (off track)
- **Benchmarks**: Compare against target, last period, or industry standard

## Anti-Patterns

- **Activity journal**: Listing everything you did without connecting to outcomes
- **Vanity metrics**: Reporting numbers that look good but don't matter
- **Surprise escalation**: Bad news should appear in reports before it becomes a crisis
- **Copy-paste**: Each report should reflect what actually happened, not be a template fill

## Checklist

- [ ] Executive summary present
- [ ] Metrics include comparison (target, previous period, trend)
- [ ] Blockers have proposed mitigations
- [ ] Next steps are specific and time-bound
- [ ] Language matches audience (technical vs. executive)
