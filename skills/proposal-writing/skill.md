# Proposal Writing Skill

## When to Use

When creating any document that requests a decision, budget, approval, or action from a decision-maker.

## Core Principles

1. **Lead with the ask**: Don't bury the request вҖ” state it in the executive summary
2. **Problem before solution**: Make the reader feel the pain before presenting the cure
3. **Quantify everything**: "$100K cost" not "significant investment"; "35% reduction" not "significant improvement"
4. **Always include alternatives**: Show you considered other options (including doing nothing)
5. **Make the cost of inaction visible**: Every proposal competes with "do nothing"

### Persuasion Flow

1. **Hook**: Why should the reader care? (Business impact, urgency, opportunity)
2. **Problem**: What's broken, missing, or at risk? (With data)
3. **Solution**: What do you propose? (Clear, specific, actionable)
4. **Evidence**: Why will this work? (Data, case studies, precedent)
5. **Ask**: What do you need? (Budget, headcount, approval, timeline)
6. **Risk mitigation**: What could go wrong and how will you handle it?

## Patterns

### "Do Nothing" Cost Calculation

Always calculate what happens if we don't act:
- **Direct costs**: Revenue lost, penalties incurred, efficiency waste
- **Opportunity costs**: What else could we do with this money/time?
- **Risk costs**: What's the probability-weighted cost of things going wrong?

### Alternative Comparison

| Dimension | Option A (Proposed) | Option B | Do Nothing |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|------------|
| Cost | ВҘX | ВҘY | ВҘ0 (but hidden cost: ВҘZ) |
| Timeline | X months | Y months | N/A |
| Risk | Medium | Low | High (growing) |
| Impact | [Specific] | [Specific] | Negative trajectory |

## Anti-Patterns

- **The novel**: Proposals over 3 pages lose executives. Be concise.
- **Vague benefits**: "Improved efficiency" вҖ” by how much? For whom? Measured how?
- **Missing the "so what"**: Connect every data point to a business outcome
- **One-sided argument**: Proposals without risks or alternatives seem naive
- **Death by appendix**: Put key info in the body, not buried in appendices

## Checklist

- [ ] Executive summary is under 100 words
- [ ] Problem is quantified with data
- [ ] Solution is specific and actionable
- [ ] At least 3 alternatives compared (including "do nothing")
- [ ] Outcomes are quantified with metrics and timeline
- [ ] Resources required are explicit (cost, people, time)
- [ ] Risks acknowledged with mitigations
- [ ] Single clear ask at the end
