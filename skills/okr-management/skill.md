# OKR Management Skill

## When to Use

When setting, tracking, or reviewing OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) or KPIs.

## OKR Anatomy

### Objective (the "what")
- Qualitative and inspiring
- Ambitious but achievable (70% completion = good)
- Time-bound (quarterly or annual)
- Example: "Become the go-to platform for agricultural data in Southeast Asia"

### Key Results (the "how to measure")
- Quantitative and measurable
- 3-5 per Objective
- Scored 0.0–1.0 (0.7 = target met, 1.0 = stretch exceeded)
- Example: "Increase MAU from 5K to 15K in Thailand by Q2"

## Writing Good Key Results

| Bad | Good | Why |
|-----|------|-----|
| "Improve customer satisfaction" | "Increase NPS from 30 to 50" | Measurable |
| "Launch new feature" | "Ship feature X with >80% adoption in first month" | Outcome, not output |
| "Reduce costs" | "Reduce infrastructure cost per user from $2.50 to $1.80" | Specific target |

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0.0–0.3 | Failed to make meaningful progress |
| 0.4–0.6 | Made progress but fell short |
| 0.7 | Delivered as planned (target) |
| 0.8–0.9 | Exceeded expectations |
| 1.0 | Extraordinary — was the goal ambitious enough? |

## Alignment

```
Company OKR
└── Team OKR (supports company OKR)
    └── Individual OKR (supports team OKR)
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Sandbagging**: Setting easy goals to guarantee 1.0 — defeats the purpose
- **Too many OKRs**: 2-3 Objectives with 3-5 KRs each. More = unfocused.
- **Output not outcome**: "Ship 10 features" → "Increase activation rate by 20%"
- **Set and forget**: OKRs need weekly/bi-weekly check-ins, not just quarterly reviews
