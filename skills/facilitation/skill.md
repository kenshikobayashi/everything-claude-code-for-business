# Facilitation Skill

## When to Use

When designing or running group discussions, workshops, or decision-making sessions.

## Convergence Techniques

### Dot Voting
Each participant gets 3-5 votes to place on their preferred options. Quickly reveals group preferences.

### Fist of Five
On count of 3, everyone shows 1-5 fingers:
- 5: Fully support
- 4: Support with minor concerns
- 3: Can live with it
- 2: Significant concerns
- 1: Block (must discuss)

### "Yes, and..." Building
Instead of "but", use "yes, and..." to build on ideas before critiquing.

### Silent Writing → Share → Group
1. Everyone writes individually (3 min)
2. Share one at a time (round-robin)
3. Group similar ideas
4. Discuss and prioritize

## Conflict Resolution

### When Two People Disagree
1. Acknowledge both perspectives
2. Find the shared interest beneath the positions
3. Ask: "What would need to be true for you to support the other option?"
4. If stuck: Propose a time-boxed experiment or pilot

### Escalation Protocol
1. Try to resolve in the meeting
2. If stuck, assign a decision-maker and deadline
3. Never leave a conflict unresolved without a clear next step

## Anti-Patterns

- **Loudest voice wins**: Use structured turn-taking
- **Endless debate**: Time-box discussions, use decision protocols
- **False consensus**: Silence ≠ agreement. Explicitly check: "Does anyone disagree?"
