# SOP Writing Skill

## When to Use

When documenting any repeatable business process so that anyone can follow it consistently.

## Core Principles

1. **Write for the newcomer**: Someone unfamiliar should be able to follow it
2. **Include exceptions**: Happy path alone is insufficient — cover what can go wrong
3. **Testable steps**: Each step should be verifiable ("Click Save" not "Save it somehow")
4. **Living document**: Include revision history and review schedule

## Structure

1. Purpose & Scope
2. Roles & Responsibilities
3. Prerequisites
4. Step-by-Step Procedure (numbered, with decision points)
5. Exception Handling
6. Quality Checklist
7. Revision History

## Step Writing Rules

- Use imperative mood: "Click the button" not "The button should be clicked"
- One action per step
- Include screenshots or examples for complex steps
- Mark decision points clearly: "If [condition], go to Step X. Otherwise, continue."
- Estimate time for each step where useful

## Anti-Patterns

- **Too abstract**: "Process the request" — what does that mean concretely?
- **Assumed knowledge**: Don't assume the reader knows your tools or jargon
- **No exceptions**: Every process has edge cases — document them
- **Set and forget**: SOPs rot fast. Schedule regular reviews.
